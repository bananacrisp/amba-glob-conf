Session Content

1 | Evidence and trends: The Global MBA Trends you need to know about

This session will outline some of the most significant trends occuring within and affecting management education globally.

Speakers

James Barker, Market Development Manager, Europe, Graduate Management Admission Council (GMAC)

David Woods–Hale, Head of Editorial and Public Relations, AMBA

2 | Key Attributes that Define a Strong MBA Candidate

This session will take the pulse of the relationship between academic thought, corporate practice and the bridges that link the two.

Our expert speakers will look at the key attributes that define strong MBA candidates and explore whether MBA programmes are adequately preparing graduates to meet corporate expectations when entering the working world. They will also consider how the hiring landscape is changing and how candidates must adapt to get noticed by employers.

Speakers

Aarti Bhasin, Employer Relations Manager, AMBA

Giuglia Gainsborough-Waring, Associate Director, Ernst & Young

3 | Digital Learning and the Role of Technology in Education

Interactive video walls, gamification and 3D virtual lecturers have become almost commonplace in Business Schools – but are these just novelties or can they help enhance online and blended MBA learning strategies?

Santiago Garcia Rodriguez, representing Grenoble Business School, which won AMBAs Annual Innovation Award earlier this year, will look the at the viability of emerging technologies, investigate and predict the future of online learning and question if the popularity of online learning has been over-stated.

Speakers

James Barker, Market Development Manager, Europe, Graduate Management Admission Council (GMAC)

David Woods–Hale, Head of Editorial and Public Relations, AMBA

4a | Key Trends and Innovations in Management Education

During this session, AMBAs Director of Development Markets will draw upon his extensive knowledge and reflect upon the key learnings and innovations observed in visits across the globe. Having taken part in accreditations in Asia, Europe and Latin America, he will give the audience a wealth of insight on best practice as well as his thoughts on where the world of business education is going.

Speakers

George Iliev, Director of Development Markets, AMBA

4b | Fundraising – the dos and don’ts

With diminishing government subsidies available for postgraduate education, fundraising is becoming an increasingly important revenue stream. This session will explore some of the facets of a successful fundraising strategy.

Speakers

Erin Hallett, Head of Alumni Relations, Imperial College Business School

4c | Recruitment Tools, Tips and Shortcuts for Reaching the Graduate and Business School Pipeline

This session will provide an overview of the vast, diverse and global GRE test-taker population and how you can segment and target this population to supplement your recruitment strategies. Attendees will leave this session more informed about the population and tactics that they can employ to find highly – qualified individuals who are the best fit for their Business School programmes

Speakers

María Victoria Calabrese, Academic and Government Relations Director, Educational Testing Service (ETS)

5 | Building Effective Communications Strategies

Communication is an extremely powerful tool, therefore knowing how to use it effectively is crucial.

Communication strategies are designed to help organisations communicate effectively and efficiently, as well as meet core organisational objectives. In this session Coca-Cola’s Anastasia Sideri will outline the key elements of a successful communications strategy and explore how Business Schools can strategically and effectively use key messaging to make sure they are engaging with all key customers, whether they be potential recruits, current students or alumni.

Speakers

Anastasia Sideri, Communications Director, The Coca-Cola Company, Central and Eastern Europe

6 | The Future Of Work and the Impact On Education

Sooner rather than later, organisations all over the globe will feel the impact of technological, demographic and socio-economic disruptions.

Boston Consulting Group analysed these disruptions and came up with 12 forces that will change how organisations work. Out of these forces, five have been identified as the ones that will have a substantial impact on educational systems.

These forces and their impact on education will be presented and the session will explore how educational systems will have to adapt, in order to shape the supply of talent in a way that it will fulfil the needs of the future.

Speakers

Melanie Seier Larsen, Senior Principal, Boston Consulting Group

7a | Globalisation and Diversifying your Cohort and Recruiting Talent

Globalisation, as a goal, is of growing importance to Business Schools. Developing more internationallyfocused curricula and attracting students from across the globe is vital for ongoing growth. During the first half of this session, Bruno Lea will look at how Business Schools can attract international talent. The speakers will then address the challenge of converting this talent onto programmes, once they’ve been attracted to your school. Karen Jacobs from Liaison International will look at how to connect driven, entrepreneurial prospective students with the programmes best suited to meet their needs.

Speakers

Bruno Lea, Associate Director, IESE Business School

Karen Jacobs, Vice President, Client Success, Liaison International

7b | The Power of Alumni Engagement

What’s the best way to leverage your alumni community? Focussing on alumni engagement to strengthen your network, can prove to be a powerful platform in your institution’s development. This session will look at how Business Schools can effectively engage with their alumni.

Speakers

Wim den Tuinder, Director, Alumni International Network, IESE Business School

8 | Activity Update

AMBA has grown faster during the past three years than it did in all its preceding 10 years put together. As AMBA celebrates its 50th Anniversary this year, our CEO Andrew Main Wilson will share our exciting plans for the next three years and present exclusive research on current and future global trend predictions in the MBA and Masters in Management markets.

Speakers

Andrew Main Wilson, CEO, AMBA

9 | Accreditation and Beyond – what AMBA can Deliver

From our free premium membership services, strong marketing and public relations campaigns, world class events and advertising templates, to our award-winning magazine, there is a range of services that AMBA provides. Here’s your opportunity to ask members of the AMBA senior management team about these services directly so they can offer innovative ways to help you and your School add value to your students and alumni.

Speakers

Leah Carter, Global Head of Events, AMBA

Martin Reid, Membership and Operations Director, AMBA

Jack Villanueva, Digital Content and Community Manager, AMBA

David Woods–Hale, Head of Editorial and Public Relations, AMBA
